Strategic Takeaways

What the data tells us about 2026 and beyond

09 / 10
  • 01Compute is the new oil. The US chip embargo has not slowed China's AI ambitions — it has accelerated domestic chip production by 40% YoY.
  • 02The talent gap is closing. China produces 4.5× more AI graduates annually. The US advantage lies in attracting the top 1% globally, a lead under geopolitical pressure.
  • 03China leads in volume, US in quality. China dominates patents and paper volume. The US leads in citation impact, foundation models, and commercial deployment.
  • 04State vs. market. China's $28B government AI budget is 2.5× the US. But US private investment ($156B) is 2× China's total. Different models, different risks.
  • 05By 2028, China may lead in foundational models. At current trajectories, China's FLOP capacity and data scale will enable frontier models competitive with GPT-6 class systems.
  • 06Global South is the next battleground. Both US and China are competing to supply AI infrastructure to Southeast Asia, Africa, and Latin America.
